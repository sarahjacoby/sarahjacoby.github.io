This is a map of fatalities in the NYC subway system. Clicking on a dot will give you a link to the accident report (pdf) that the data for that fatality came from.

According to the MTA, over 1.5 billion people ride the subway every year. And, according to MTA data obtained by the New York Post this June, about 50 people of those 1.5 billion die in the subway annually. Suicides, usually classified as "non passenger" deaths attributed to "unsafe actions," are the most common form of subway death. The data used to create this map is from accident reports collected and published online by the NY Department of Transportation. But because there are only 49 fatality reports available online, we know this data set is currently incomplete. However, this data does include both passenger (blue) and non passenger (red) fatalities. Two thirds of the fatalities recorded here were non passenger fatalities.

There are 21 subway lines. These are the deadliest ones, each with 4 fatalities:

Local between the Bronx and lower Manhattan.
Lexington Av express between Manhattan and Brooklyn.
Lexington Av local operates between the Bronx and lower Manhattan.
Local along 8th Av line to Jamaica Center in Queens.
Runs local between lower Manhattan and Jamaica Center.
Local train running between Manhattan and northern Brooklyn, briefly through Queens.

Of the 468 subway stations, these are the deadliest stops. With so many stops, deaths are pretty evenly distributed across them. Only these stops had more than one fatality:

  • Tremont Av Served by the B and D lines in the Bronx.
  • Franklin Av The IRT Eastern Parkway line (2, 3, 4, 5) all stop here.
  • Brooklyn Bridge - City Hall The 4 and 6 both stop here.
  • 5th Av/59th St The only stop on the N-Q-R line on the dangerous lists.
  • 14th St The E and L lines both stop here (also the A and C).
  • 110th St Like the Brooklyn Bridge - City Hall stop, this one is also served by the 4 and 6 trains.
